01

A note from the founders

Modern industry was built by mastering chemical manufacturing — from ingredients and materials to medicines. The next manufacturing platform will be biological: more sustainable, more programmable, and better suited to a resource-constrained world.

But biology will not scale as an industrial platform unless it becomes controllable. And it will not become controllable unless it becomes predictable.

That is why Lemnisca exists. We are building predictive models and engineering workflows for biomanufacturing, so teams can understand, optimize, and scale biological processes with greater confidence.

Over the last six months, we built the operating base to pursue this: a cross-functional team, incubation and lab access, fermentation infrastructure, computational engineering capability, customer-facing solutions, and initial customer work.

We're still early. But the risk profile has changed: we have moved from thesis-building to proof-building. The next phase is focused on structured datasets, stronger models, repeatable customer programs, and scale-up insights that customers can trust and act on.

— Dr. Pushkar Pendse & Dr. Shilpa Nargund, Co-founders

07

Technology stack

Lemnisca is building a wet-lab and dry-lab stack for predictive bioprocess scale-up.

01

Experimental layer

Wet lab

Shake-flask and fermentation workflows are being designed around scale-relevant variables: media, feeds, pH, temperature, agitation, aeration, sampling plans, process trajectories, and assay outputs.

02

Data layer

Wet + dry

Experimental and process data are being structured across recipes, biological system metadata, operating conditions, equipment context, time-series signals, offline assays, deviations, observations, and batch outcomes.

03

Modeling layer

Dry lab

Dry-lab workflows span molecular simulation, pathway and kinetic modeling, reactor mass transfer, mixing, oxygen-transfer analysis, CFD, scale-up/scale-down models, and technoeconomic modeling.

04

AI decision layer

AI

ML, optimization, and agentic AI workflows are being built to learn from data, calibrate models, prioritize experiments, map scale-up risks, and support customer decisions.
